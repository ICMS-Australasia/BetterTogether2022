World Cerebral Palsy Day

Wednesday 6 October 2021

As cerebral palsy affects more than 17 million people worldwide, there are millions of reasons to make your voice heard on October 6. Join the movement this World Cerebral Palsy day to help create awareness and action around cerebral palsy.

World Listening and Sharing Event

What have you got planned for World CP Day? Join the AusACPDM at the upcoming Free Listening and Sharing Event on the special topic “Diagnosis vs Functional thinking”.  This session will involve a panel of health professionals and consumers discussing “Diagnosis vs functional thinking” in how we promote excellence in clinical care and research for children and adults with cerebral palsy and developmental conditions.

Late Breaking Abstracts

The Scientific Committee invites you to submit abstracts now for late breaking free papers.

Submissions are welcomed over a wide range of themes including but not limited to:

  • Adults and transitional care
  • Care in lower resource settings
  • Communication and assistive devices
  • Consumer engagement
  • Early detection, neonatal care and early interventions
  • Family/caregiver health and wellbeing
  • Genomics
  • Indigenous and First Nations people and disability
  • Knowledge translation and implementation
  • Living and planning for pandemics and disasters
  • Mental health and wellbeing
  • Movement disorders
  • Neuroscience and neuroimaging
  • New technologies and innovations
  • Nutrition
  • Orthopaedics, muscle and bone health
  • Pain
  • Participation
  • Physical activity
  • Preclinical studies
  • Sleep
  • Telehealth
  • Vision

The deadline to submit your Late Breaking Abstract is: 23:59 (AEDT) Wednesday 1 December 2021, get in now and don’t miss out.
