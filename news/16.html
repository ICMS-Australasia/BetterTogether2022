World Mental Health Day

Sunday 10 October 2021

The overall objective of World Mental Health Day is to raise awareness of mental health issues around the world and to mobilise efforts in support of mental health.

“Look up, look out & look forward” - Mental Health Australia

Due to COVID-19 the Better Together 2022 Organising Committee made the decision to move to a fully virtual Conference in March 2022. This is a challenging time with lockdowns, social distancing and border closures and we understand that although this Conference is fully virtual, considerations need to be made to ensure peoples’ health and wellbeing.

We are pleased to provide an insight into our planning and announce the Conferences’ new initative The Feel Good Corner! This will be an exciting resource gallery to assist in looking after yourself both mentally and physically during the Conference, as well as having the opportunity to ‘virtually’ visit Melbourne and its surrounds.

Keep an eye out for the Feel Good Pop-up Sessions, there is one each day!

Registration Scholarships

For people with disability and their supporters

The Better Together 2022 Organising Committee is pleased to offer Registration Scholarships for people with disability and their family members or caregivers.

This Conference brings together health professionals, researchers and people with lived experience of disability to share knowledge, do research, develop policies and advocate for better care to allow people with childhood onset disability to actively participate in community life.

We are committed to collaborating with people with disability to ensure our program is relevant and has impact. To support involvement of the disability community we are offering Registration Scholarships to attend this virtual event.

Better Together 2022 will provide the perfect forum for companies actively involved in cerebral palsy and childhood-onset disability to promote their products and services.
